A thick fog, the color of stale ale, clung to the cobbled streets of Whitechapel, each gas lamp a feeble yellow eye struggling to pierce the gloom. The air hung heavy with the stench of coal smoke and something else, something metallic and sharp that made Inspector Davies's nostrils twitch. It wasn't the usual reek of the tanneries or the slaughterhouses; this was a different kind of foulness, the kind that clung to the back of the throat and whispered of secrets best left buried.
